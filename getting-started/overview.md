# Overview — What is Kirra?

**Kirra** is a desktop blast design application built for drill-and-blast engineers, blasting contractors, and mine site technical staff. It provides a complete workflow from importing drilled hole data, through pattern design and charge deck configuration, all the way to exporting production-ready blast packages.

---

## Who Is Kirra For?

| Role | How Kirra Helps |
|------|-----------------|
| **Blast Engineer** | Design and optimise blast patterns, assign timing sequences, and produce compliant export files |
| **Drill & Blast Contractor** | Import drill hole surveys, build charge decks, and export to downstream systems quickly |
| **Mine Geotechnician** | Analyse vibration exposure with the PPV shader and review Voronoi rock distribution |
| **Shotfirer / Blaster** | Review hole-by-hole charge instructions and timing diagrams before initiating |

---

## What Kirra Does

### Core Capabilities

- **Pattern layout** — manually place holes or generate rectangular, staggered, polygon, and polyline-based patterns
- **Drill data import** — bring in CSV, DXF, Surpac STR, and Epiroc iRedes XML formats
- **Hole properties** — capture depth, diameter, subdrill, bearing, inclination, and custom attributes per hole
- **Charging module** — build deck-loaded charge designs with stemming, boosters, and explosive products from your own product database
- **Timing sequences** — assign initiation delays, tie-ins, and surface connections across the pattern
- **Analysis** — view blast statistics, Voronoi rock coverage, and PPV vibration shading
- **Export** — produce Kirra CSV, DXF, Epiroc XML, MineStar AQM, and PDF reports
